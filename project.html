The Coral Project

THE GIST

The Coral Project is an open-source collaboration with The Washington Post, The New York Times and Mozilla. After receiving a four million dollar grant in 2015, The Coral Project built a suite of products which allow journalists to better engage with their readers and vice versa.

I joined the team as the Lead Designer in February 2016 and oversaw the visual design, information architecture and execution of our design team of two’s human-centered design process.

Given the small size of the team, each member had the unique opportunity to dive deep and work in a variety of roles throughout planning and execution. I worked with independent researchers to conduct remote user testing and learn more about our users. Shortly after I joined the team, I also helped hire our User Experience Designer Sam. Together, she and I prioritized putting the user first at every step of the process from sketching to user testing.

Working in a remote environment with developers, a product manager, and project leads taught me the importance of over communicating and speaking up during both regular and impromptu meetings. We adopted an agile workflow which encouraged team-wide collaboration despite the hundreds of miles between us.

Agile also helped us position our design team for success since we created design ahead of development sprints. I also led bi-weekly design thinking exercises remotely in an effort to cultivate empathy for our users and challenge conventional notions about online communities.

Every team member’s belief in our mission to create better online communities helped to push our project further than we could have imagined, and I am proud to have been part of this team.

Currently, I’m applying everything I learned while part of The Coral Project to our online communities at The Washington Post.
